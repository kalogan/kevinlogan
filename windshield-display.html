The Challenge

Our team was asked to create a conceptual design of Volkswagen's self-driving car of the future. Our goal was to conceptualize a new interaction that could take place in fifteen years.

Our Solution


In fifteen years, we want to simulate the workstation in the car. We aim to help people complete work related tasks by creating an office space in their vehicle. The car will be fully autonomous on the highway. This design will provide Volkswagen a solution they can realistically implement in the upcoming future.

The workstation will consist primarily of a display on the windshield. The windshield is used because users in the future will still be sitting in the front seat with a steering wheel. This will allow users to check emails, attend conference calls, browse the internet, and read their files. The user will simply interact with the workstation by using voice and gestures.

User Research

We conducted secondary research as inspiration to uncover the different design possibilites of the future on a car that can drive itself. Next, we decided to select a target audience; we chose adults who commuted more than 45 minutes on the highway to work.

Our team then interviewed people who fit this description to understand their pain points, needs, and motivations. In addition, we conducted a survey sorrounding people's behavior while driving. We uncovered that they didn't want to waste their time commuting, they wanted to start work while they were driving. Our team used this information as inspiration for our design.

Idea Generation & Sketches


Our goal was to help our users complete work on their way to work. During this phase, we used Golden Questions to assist us. We came up with several different concepts and sketched them out. We critiqued and analyzed all of them until we came up with a concept we liked. We decided upon a windshield display as the concept we wanted to pursue. Sketching helped us figure out what features we wanted to include in the display.

Interface Design

We came up with a paper prototype of the windshield display. This paper prototype allowed us to make subtle changes when necessary. This prototype allowed us to visualize our designs and come up with distinct interactions. We then conducted a usability test to see if this was understandable and usable by our target audience.
