The Challenge

Our team was asked to design a product or service for a target audience that is often forgetten or left behind. We took it upon ourselves to design a new experience for street musicians!

Our Solution

Our team designed a kiosk for street musicians. This kiosk allows audience members to purchase songs, donate, and learn more about the musician.

User Research

We conducted skype interviews with three street musicians from New York and Chicago. These interviews helped us to understand their pain points and frustrations. In addition, we ran a survey to understand people's attitudes about street performers. Here is a list of our findings:

1.) Street performers were often viewed poorly by the public.
2.) Street performers were losing money because people often did not carry cash.
3.) They had difficulty finding the perfect location to perform to maximize profits.

Picture of Hannah Watson, found on her website

Idea Generation & Sketches


Our team made sure to make use of our research findings. We decided to help change the perception the public had of them. In order to do that, we needed to make the performers look more professional and legitimate. Our goal was to help these performers out through the assistance of technology. The device we wanted to create would also help them sell their songs to help them make money and become famous. Our team sketched several different ideas before settling on this design.

Interface Design

We first started with a paper prototype because it allowed room for suggestions. When we designed this interface, we had to make it very simple and easy to follow. Most people who watch street performers are only there for a few songs at most. We only included four primary features: Tip the performer, buy their songs, learn about them, and hire them.

Usability Testing

We conducted 3 usability tests on our paper prototype by using the RITE method. The RITE method allowed us to have a mutual understanding of what should be easily used by users and allow us to rapidly iterate on it. The only issues we found with our design were the semantics of our phrases. Finally, we presented this design to the street musicians and they greeted the design with positive thoughts.
