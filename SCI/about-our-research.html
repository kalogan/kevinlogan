About Our Research

Travel X is a research project focused on making it easier for disabled travelers to find travel accesssibility information. To be specific, we are hoping to help people with Spinal Cord Injury.

Spinal cord injury (SCI) usually produces sensory and/or mobility impairment, which often makes it more difficult for persons living with SCI to fully participate in society. In mainstream U.S.A., full participation in community and society requires people to travel to some degree for work, school and leisure. In many cases, however, travel may pose serious challenges for persons after SCI.

Guided by the Self-Determination Theory, this study aims to identify facilitators of and barriers to participation of people living with SCI in travel-related activities, and to understand how these factors enhance or impede participation through travel needs satisfaction and motivation. The ability to travel, as a common thread in independent living, employment and community living, is highly relevant to participation in many life’s activities. Thus, the study’s findings can be used to improve the quality of life for people living with SCI.
